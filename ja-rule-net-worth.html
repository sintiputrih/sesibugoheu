    Grammy award-winning American rapper, singer, songwriter, and actor Ja Rule’s net worth is $4 million.

    Ja is Best known for his chart-topping songs such as Between Me and You, I’m Real, Mesmerize, Wonderful, and many more.

    Some of you might know him for his infamous feud with fellow rapper 50 Cent.

    Similarly, he has collaborated with some A-lister singers such as R.Kelly, Jenifer Lopez, and Ashanti.

    Ja has won four Grammy Awards with his collaborators Lil’ Mo, Ashanti, Case, and Vita.

    Likewise, he has some Platinum-certified records to his name. His debut album Venni Vetti Vecci was certified Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).

    He has sold over 30 million records worldwide and 14.4 million units in the US alone.

    His career skyrocketed when he released Rule 3:36 and Pain Is Love which was his second and third albums; both of the albums topped the US Billboard 200 chart.

    The album sold over a combined 15 million records worldwide and was certified triple Platinum by RIAA.

    After that, he released a couple of Gold-certified albums.

    In 2019 he combined with the main cast of  TV’s Growing Up Hip Hop: New York.

    Early Life

    Ja Rule, originally Jeffery Bruce Atkins, was born on February the 29th, 1976, in Hollis, a section of Queens, New York City, US.

    His father, William, left the family when Ja was still a child, and his mother, Debra, was a healthcare worker.

    Debra was really busy, and due to the amount she spent working, Ja was largely raised by his grandparents as a Jehovah’s Witness, which is a religion.

    Ja was the only child as his younger sister Kristen died in the womb when Ja was five years of age.

    Just like any other rapper, he was involved in drug dealing from a really young age.

    Career

    Ja Rule began his career in 1994 as a member of Cash Money Click that produced songs independently.

    The group was signed to TVT records but dropped as they disbanded after just one year due to the arrest of one of the group members.

    Ja Rule’s first solo appearance was “Time to build.” The song was by Mic Geronimo and also featured DMX and Jay-Z.

    In 1998 Ja signed with Def Jam records. He was even featured in Jay-Z’s song “Can I Get A….”

    His debut album was released in 1999 named “Venni Vetti Vecci,” which topped the Billboard 200.

    In 2005 Ja’s contract with Def Jam expired.

    He announced that he was coming up with his album “Pain is Love” in 2011 but was released a year later. But after that, he went to jail for two years and was released in May 2013.

    Following the prison release, Ja Rule and Glotti launched Murder Inc. Records. He also announced his eighth studio album but was pushed back.
