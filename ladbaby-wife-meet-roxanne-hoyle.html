    LadBaby wife-British blogger, YouTuber and social media personality, LadBaby was born on April 12th, 1987 in Nottingham, United Kingdom.

    His videos, which he produces with his wife Roxanne Zee Hoyle, center on his fathering experiences.

    In 2018, 2019, 2020, and 2021, the couple’s novelty songs “We Built This City,” “I Love Sausage Rolls,” “Don’t Stop Me Eatin’,” and “Sausage Rolls for Everyone” consecutively, topped the UK Singles Chart for Christmas.

    LadBaby matched The Beatles’ record of four nonconsecutive Christmas number ones in 1963, 1964, 1965, and 1967 by being the first artist in UK history to secure four consecutive Christmas number ones and the second act to do so.

    The LadBaby Facebook page, Instagram account, and YouTube channel, respectively, have more than 1 million subscribers, 4.9 million followers, and 1.3 million followers as of December 2020.

    The majority of the videos produced by LadBaby and posted to their Facebook and YouTube pages are vlogs in which Mark discusses money-saving tips he uses to handle potentially expensive parenting duties. In a video blog that went viral in June 2017, the man explained how he bought a toolbox from a hardware store to use as his son’s lunchbox.

    He has also constructed a baby walker out of pipes, a paddling pool out of a skip, and a baby gate out of the bottom part of a door.

    The Hoyle pair decorated each other’s automobiles with love hearts and images of their faces in two other viral films that were posted in February 2018.

    Yes, mate! is another saying made famous by LadBaby and is frequently used in the videos. The Hoyles started a separate YouTube channel called LadBaby & Sons in October 2018 where their sons play a more prominent role.

    LadBaby stated in December 2021 that they were attempting to secure a fourth consecutive holiday Number 1 with the release of a new Christmas song starring Elton John and Ed Sheeran.

    The new song “Sausage Rolls For Everyone” is based on a 2021 number one, “Merry Christmas,” which peaked at number one on the Official Singles Chart Top 100 on December 10, 2021, with featured singers Ed Sheeran and Elton John (week ending date 16 December 2021).

    Once more, LadBaby discovered that the new album was competing with singles by Adele and Gayle, as well as older songs by Wham! and Mariah Carey (both of whom had number one successes the year before).

    The Hoyles, stroke survivor Donna Kennedy, and her 12-year-old son Ronan are all featured in a new video for the acoustic version that was shot at St Margaret’s the Queen Church (where the Norwood and Brixton Food Bank is located).

    When the original “Merry Christmas” replaced “Sausage Rolls For Everyone” at the top of the charts on December 31, 2021, “Sausage Rolls For Everyone” fell to number 29 in the charts, spending a second week in the UK Top 40, and matching the fall of the Lewisham and Greenwich NHS Choir’s “A Bridge Over You” from 2015, which once experienced the top-to-bottom fall that set records.

    The comedy group The Kunts has questioned the motives behind LadBaby’s holiday popularity, even going so far as to claim that they are making money off of the poor.

    Meet LadBaby’s wife, Roxanne Hoyle

    LadBaby is married to Roxanne Hoyle. They got married in May 2015. They have been blessed with two kids; Phoenix Forest and Kobe Notts.
