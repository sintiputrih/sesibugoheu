    Brad Ludden: Career

    Firstly, his parents gave him all the support and his first kayak when he was nine.

    Secondly, at age 12, he was already traveling and competing internationally.

    As a result, by 18 years of age, he had traveled to over 20 countries for kayaking and was sponsored by Nike.

    Above all, after traveling to several locations, he found his true passion within the sport, with his first descents.

    First descents refer to the first time a person successfully kayaks a river or section of a river. In addition, it has to have never been done before.

    After that, he began to perform numerous first descents. Consequently, he found an immense challenge, adventure, and personal growth from the sport.

    As a result, a large part of Brad’s identity became tied to his pursuit of first descents.

    Brad has kayaked for Teva, Dagger, AT Paddles, Kokatat, and Smith Optics in his journey as a professional kayaker.

    However, he is mainly recognized for his runs with the dagger as his sporting partner.

    First Descents

    First Descent is a non-profit charitable organization that provides outdoor adventures for young adults impacted by cancer.

    In addition, it also helps patients with other severe health conditions.

    Brad Ludden founded First Descents at the age of 20.

    Establishment

    Despite his successful kayaking journey, Ludden felt something missing in his life.

    Additionally, he wanted to share the experience of outdoor adventure with others who needed it more.

    Initially, first descents was something related only to the sport for Ludden.

    Similarly, Cancer was also just another word for Brad until his aunt was diagnosed with breast cancer at 38.

    After his beloved aunt’s breast cancer diagnosis when he was 12, his mother began to volunteer work at a local pediatric oncology camp.

    In addition, she asked him to join her for the volunteer work. Indeed, Brad did.

    As a result of volunteering in the camps and watching his aunt fight the disease, he decided to do more at 18.

    Hence, he decided to share his passion for kayaking with people suffering from various diseases.

    Because he knew kayaking was filled with joy and freedom, he believed it had much to offer to young people facing terrifying diagnoses.

    Moreover, Kayaking provided the empowerment of learning a new challenging skill and the splendor of nature.

    After realizing he could help people while continuing his kayaking, he began constructing First Descent in 1999 and finished in 2001.

    20 years and counting

    It is now 20 years since the founding of First Descents. During its 20-year history, the charity has run over a thousand programs nationwide.

    As a result, this organization has been able to serve nearly ten thousand young adult survivors.

    Moreover, the charity has established relationships with more than 150 medical centers nationwide.

    In other words, they are now able to reach patients closer to the time of diagnosis effectively.

    Meanwhile, in 2020, the charity also expanded the program to serve healthcare professionals on the frontlines of COVID-19.

    As a result of founding the charity and its success, Brad Ludden won a CNN hero award in 2016.
