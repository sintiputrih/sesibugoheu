    Evan Spiegel | Net Worth & Income

    Net Worth

    A French-American businessman Evan Spiegel’s net worth as of December 2023 is a whopping $3.2 billion.

    All of his wealth is a result of Snap Inc., which he co-founded in 2011.

    As mentioned before, he became the youngest billionaire in 2015 at the age of 25.

    In 2013, Evan Spiegel turned down a $3 billion cash buyout offer from Facebook.

    You read that right, a 23 years old founder who created an app that made no money at the time and was in its second year of operation just casually turned down a staggering $3 billion acquisition offer from the tech giant Facebook.

    He turned down the offer because, in some weird way, he already knew that the app he had created was worth way more than Facebook’s $3 billion.

    Let’s not forget Yahoo offered $1 billion to acquire Facebook back in 2006 and see where it is now.

    The history repeated in 2013, and turning down the 10-figure paycheck from Facebook was the best decision Evan ever made.

    The company has an annual revenue of over $4 billion and is worth $36.77 billion in total.

    Who knew?

    Evan Spiegel, that’s who!

    In August 2014, Venture Capital firm Kleiner Perkins valued the company at $10 billion.

    The company went public and debuted on the New York Stock Exchange on March 2, 2017.

    While the forecasted per share opening price was $17, to everyone’s surprise, it opened at $24.

    Evan sold 16 million shares of the company, which garnered him $272 million pretax and roughly $180 million after-tax.

    As of this writing, Evan still owns roughly 211 million shares on Snap Inc., and with today’s trading price of $13.28 per share, that’s more than $2.8 billion.

    Again it’s just a rough calculation.

    Evan Spiegel | Career

    As mentioned before, Evan was born in an affluent household to lawyer parents.

    He grew up in Pacific Palisades, California, which is one of the exclusive neighborhoods in the United States.

    After graduating high school, he enrolled at Stanford University. He met his future business partners Reggie Brown and Bobby Murphy at the university.
