    What is Labour Day?

    Labour Day is an annual holiday in both Canada and the United States that celebrates the achievements of workers.

    "Labour Day is more than a holiday. It is a day to honour the workers and the entire labour movement that helped shape Canada for the better," Seamus O’Regan Jr., the Minister of Labour, said in a statement on September 4, 2023.

    "This is the movement that made weekends and lunch breaks a reality. It is the movement that created the middle class we have today.

    "...For more than 150 years, Canada’s labour movement has been fighting for safer, fairer work. Today organized labour is still raising the bar on what a good job and a good workplace are. Our government is proud to work with Canadian labour organizations to deliver for workers."

    At first, Labour Day was celebrated in the spring, but it was later moved to the fall after the Parliament of Canada made it a legal holiday in 1894.
